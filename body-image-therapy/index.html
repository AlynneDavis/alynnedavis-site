Body Image Therapy in Charlotte, NC

What you think about your body didn’t come from nowhere. Let’s find out where it came from — and build something kinder in its place.

Body Image Is About So Much More Than How You Look

Body image isn’t just about appearance. It’s about how safe you feel in your own skin. It’s about how much mental space your body takes up — and whether that’s space you chose, or space it took on its own. It’s about whether you can move through a day, a meal, a mirror without cataloguing your flaws.

Poor body image shrinks your life. It keeps you out of the pool, out of photos, out of intimacy. It turns the body into an ongoing problem to manage rather than a home to live in.

I’m Alynne Davis, LCMHC, a body image therapist in Charlotte, NC. I help women understand where their body story comes from — culture, family, trauma, loss, life transitions — and begin building a genuinely different relationship with themselves. This work isn’t about learning to love every part of your body. It’s about making peace with being in it.

Who This Is For

You might be looking for a body image therapist in Charlotte if any of this sounds familiar:

  • You spend meaningful time criticizing what you see in the mirror
  • You avoid situations — the beach, photos, intimacy — because of how you feel in your body
  • Your self-worth rises and falls with the scale, or with how you “look” on any given day
  • You’ve lost weight and still don’t feel good — because the problem was never the weight
  • Your body feels like something to manage, fix, or fight rather than something to live in
  • Diet culture has been a constant inner presence for as long as you can remember
  • A health event, pregnancy, or life change has shifted your relationship with your body
  • You’re exhausted by how much mental energy this takes

Body image distress isn’t vanity. It’s suffering — and it’s worth taking seriously.

What’s Often Driving Body Image Distress

Poor body image rarely comes from one place. For most women, it’s a layered story:

Diet Culture and Media Messaging

Decades of exposure to images and messages that narrowly define acceptable bodies leave a residue that doesn’t wash off with awareness alone. Even women who “know better” intellectually still carry the internal critic that diet culture installed. We work on dismantling those internalized messages and building a more grounded sense of self.

Trauma Stored in the Body

Sexual trauma, medical trauma, and early experiences of being sexualized or scrutinized can leave the body feeling like an unsafe place to live. When trauma is part of the body image story — and it often is — we address it directly. Read more about trauma therapy for women and how trauma connects to body and eating patterns.

Family and Relational Messages

The things said — and unsaid — about bodies in your family of origin leave marks. Whether it was explicit criticism, comparison, or simply the modeling of women who hated their own bodies, these early messages often become the voice in your head. Therapy helps you identify where that voice came from and decide whether you want to keep listening to it.

Perfectionism and Achievement-Based Worth

For women who tie their value to performance and achievement, the body often becomes another metric to optimize. The body becomes a project that’s never finished. We explore what self-worth rooted in something other than productivity and appearance can actually feel like.

Eating Disorder Patterns

Restriction, binge eating, and disordered eating both feed and are fed by body image distress. If eating patterns are part of the picture, we address those directly — this work is closely connected to eating disorder therapy and often happens alongside it.

Life Transitions

Pregnancy, postpartum, aging, illness, and other significant life changes can disrupt the relationship a woman has with her body even if it was previously functional. These transitions deserve real attention — not just reassurance that the body “bounced back.”

Therapy Approaches I Use

Expressive Arts Therapy

Body image is deeply visual and deeply felt — and it often lives in places that words alone can’t reach. Expressive arts therapy offers other ways in: through drawing, imagery, writing, and creative process, we give the body story somewhere to go besides the same looping internal criticism. This can open unexpected doors to self-understanding and to a different kind of self-relationship.

Somatic and Body-Based Approaches

The body is not just the object of the work — it’s also a participant in it. Somatic approaches help you build a different kind of relationship with your physical experience: one based on curiosity and attunement rather than evaluation and control. We use breath, sensation awareness, and body-based tools to help you begin to inhabit yourself with more ease.

IFS — Internal Family Systems

IFS offers a way to understand the inner critic that constantly evaluates your body — to meet it with compassion rather than more war. Often the part that hates the body is protecting something: a wound, a fear, a younger version of you that received a painful message. IFS helps us get to that layer, and begin healing it from the inside out.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT helps interrupt the thought patterns that keep body image distress in motion — the comparisons, the catastrophizing, the all-or-nothing evaluations. We work to build more balanced, accurate, and flexible ways of thinking about yourself and your body. Not toxic positivity. Just less cognitive cruelty.

What Working Together Looks Like

Your First Session

Your first session is a space to share your story. I’ll ask questions and listen closely, and we’ll begin to get a sense of what’s been happening and what you’re hoping for. There’s no agenda to push through. Just a real conversation about where you are and what might help.

Weekly Sessions

Sessions are 50 minutes, typically weekly. Body image work often moves slowly and nonlinearly — which is exactly how it should. We build a container steady enough to hold the complexity of this, and we work inside it together, at a pace that actually lets things shift.

In Person & Telehealth

I practice in Charlotte, NC and offer telehealth to clients throughout North Carolina and South Carolina. Many women doing this kind of work find that telehealth offers a layer of comfort — you’re in your own space, and there’s no commute after a session that went somewhere meaningful.

Free 15-Minute Consultation

A free consultation is available before you commit to anything. It’s a chance to ask questions, share a little about where you are, and see if we feel like a good fit. No pressure. Just a conversation.
