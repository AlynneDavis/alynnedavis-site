Privacy Policy

How Alynne Davis, PLLC collects, uses, and protects your personal information.

Information We Collect

When you visit our website or use our contact form, we may collect the following types of information:

Information You Provide Directly

  • Name — provided when you submit our contact form.
  • Email address — provided when you submit our contact form or correspond with us.
  • Phone number — optionally provided through our contact form.
  • Message content — any information you choose to include in the message field of our contact form.

Information Collected Automatically

  • Website usage data — such as pages visited, time spent on pages, referring URLs, and browser type.
  • Device information — including device type, operating system, and screen resolution.
  • IP address — collected automatically when you access our website.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Respond to inquiries — to reply to messages submitted through our contact form and follow up on your questions about therapy services.
  • Schedule appointments — to coordinate consultations and therapy sessions.
  • Improve our website — to understand how visitors use our site and enhance the user experience.
  • Communicate important updates — to share relevant information about our practice, services, or policies when appropriate.

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes.

HIPAA Compliance

Alynne Davis, PLLC is a licensed therapy practice, and all therapy sessions and clinical records are protected under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). We take the privacy of our clients’ protected health information (PHI) very seriously and maintain strict safeguards in accordance with federal and state regulations.

Please note: This website is informational in nature and is not a patient portal. Information submitted through the contact form on this website is not considered protected health information under HIPAA. Please do not submit sensitive health information through the website contact form. Once you become a client, all clinical communications will occur through HIPAA-compliant channels.

Cookies and Analytics

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to improve your browsing experience. Currently, our site uses:

  • Google Fonts — to load and display web fonts. When you visit our site, your browser may connect to Google’s servers to retrieve font files. Google may collect limited data (such as your IP address) as part of this process. You can review Google’s Privacy Policy for more information.
  • Essential cookies — used to ensure the basic functionality of our website, such as navigation and access to secure areas.

We may implement additional analytics tools in the future to better understand site traffic and visitor behavior. If we do, this policy will be updated to reflect those changes.

Third-Party Services

We may use third-party services to support the operation of our website and practice. These may include:

  • Contact form processing — submissions through our contact form may be processed and stored using third-party services to facilitate communication.
  • Hosting and database services — we may use services such as Supabase or similar platforms for data storage and website functionality.
  • Font delivery — Google Fonts is used to serve typefaces on our website.

These third-party services have their own privacy policies governing the use and handling of your data. We encourage you to review their policies as well.

Data Security

We take reasonable and appropriate measures to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Secure, encrypted connections (HTTPS/SSL) for all website traffic.
  • Restricted access to personal information to authorized personnel only.
  • Regular review and updating of our security practices.
  • Use of HIPAA-compliant platforms for all clinical communications and records.

While we strive to protect your personal information, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but are committed to maintaining industry-standard protections.

Your Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access — you may request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction — you may request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal information.
  • Deletion — you may request that we delete your personal information, subject to any legal or regulatory obligations that require us to retain certain records.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided below. We will respond to your request in a timely manner.

Children’s Privacy

Our website is not directed at children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal information from a child under 13, we will take steps to delete that information as promptly as possible. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make changes, we will update the “Last Updated” date at the bottom of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically to stay informed about how we are protecting your information.
