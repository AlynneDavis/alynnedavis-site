Trauma-Informed Therapy for Women in Charlotte, NC

I offer trauma-informed expressive arts therapy for women in Charlotte who are navigating trauma, perfectionism, body shame, and complex relationships with food or control. My work is relational, body-centered, and paced to support safety and self-trust — especially for women who feel unseen or disconnected from themselves.

A Relational, Body-Centered Approach to Healing

Healing doesn't happen through insight alone. In our work together, we pay attention to your body, emotions, and inner experience — not to fix or override them, but to listen with curiosity and care. This approach supports nervous system regulation, self-understanding, and a deeper sense of connection with yourself over time.

When Coping and Control Are No Longer Working

Many women come to therapy after years of holding it together — managing, achieving, or controlling to feel safe. You may struggle with perfectionism, chronic self-monitoring, anxiety around food or your body, or a constant sense of pressure to "do better." Therapy provides a space to soften these patterns and explore what lies beneath them, at a pace that feels supportive rather than overwhelming.

Without the right tools or support, life can begin to feel like a series of endless hurdles, each one harder to overcome than the last.
