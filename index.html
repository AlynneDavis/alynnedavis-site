Does Any of This Sound Familiar?

You may be used to holding everything together on the outside while quietly struggling on the inside.

  • You feel disconnected from your body or unsure how to trust yourself.

A Different Way of Healing

My work blends trauma-informed therapy, expressive arts therapy, and nervous system support to help women reconnect with themselves at a deeper level.

Many of the women I work with have spent years trying to push through their struggles or make themselves "better" through willpower alone. Healing often begins when we slow down and become curious about the patterns shaping our lives.

Through a combination of therapeutic conversation, creative exploration, and nervous system awareness, therapy becomes a place where you can understand yourself with more compassion and clarity.

This process is not about fixing what is broken. It is about reconnecting with the parts of you that have been carrying too much for too long.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if therapy is right for me?

If you feel stuck in patterns that are painful or confusing and want support in understanding yourself more deeply, therapy can help.

What does expressive arts therapy involve?

Expressive arts therapy integrates creative practices such as drawing, writing, movement, and reflection to help access emotions and insights that may be difficult to express through words alone.

Do you offer therapy in Charlotte, North Carolina?

Yes. I provide trauma-informed expressive arts therapy for women in Charlotte and surrounding communities.

Do I need to be artistic to benefit from expressive arts therapy?

Not at all. Expressive arts therapy is not about artistic skill. It is about exploration, creativity, and connecting with your inner experience in new ways.

What issues do you specialize in?

I specialize in working with women navigating eating disorders, trauma, ADHD, body image struggles, perfectionism, and patterns of coping or control that impact their relationship with themselves.

What Happens After You Schedule a Consultation

  1. Choose a time that works for you. Use the scheduling link to select a time for your free consultation.
  2. We will talk for about 15 minutes. This is simply a chance for you to share what is bringing you to therapy and ask any questions you may have.
  3. We will see if it feels like a good fit. Therapy works best when there is a sense of safety and connection. If it feels right, we can discuss next steps.
  4. There is no pressure to commit. The consultation is simply an opportunity to explore whether working together feels supportive for you.
