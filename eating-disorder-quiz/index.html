Is My Relationship With Food Normal?

It’s a question a lot of women ask — quietly, privately, wondering if what they experience around food is just “how everyone feels” or something worth paying attention to.

This free self-reflection guide explores five areas where eating struggles commonly show up. Check what resonates, and receive a personalized reflection at the end — not a diagnosis, just clarity.

Explore Your Relationship With Food

Read through each area below and check the experiences that feel true to you — even a little bit. You can check as many or as few as you’d like.

01

Control & Restriction

Some women relate to food through a set of rules — lists of “good” and “bad” foods, calorie limits, or restrictions that feel necessary to stay in control. What starts as structure can quietly become a cage.

 
02

Emotional Eating & Food as Comfort

For many women, food becomes one of the most reliable ways to cope with emotions that feel too big to sit with. Stress, loneliness, anxiety, boredom — food can quiet all of it, at least for a moment.

 
03

Feeling Out of Control Around Food

Binge eating isn’t about willpower — it’s often the nervous system’s response to deprivation, stress, or emotional overload. The cycle of feeling out of control around food is exhausting, and most people carry it completely alone.

 
04

Body Image & Self-Worth

How you feel about your body can shape everything — what you wear, where you go, how you show up in relationships. When your sense of worth is tied to your body, it’s worth paying close attention.

 
05

Food Thoughts & Mental Preoccupation

When food and eating take up most of your mental energy — even when you’re not eating — it affects your ability to be present, to focus, to enjoy your life. You might not even realize how much space it takes up until you stop and look.
