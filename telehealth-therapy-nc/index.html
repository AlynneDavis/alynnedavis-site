Telehealth Therapy for Women in North Carolina

Real therapy. Real connection. From wherever you are in North Carolina or South Carolina.

Book a Free Consultation

Online Therapy That Actually Feels Like Therapy

Telehealth isn’t a lesser version of in-person therapy. For many women, it’s actually the preferred format — no commute after a hard session, the ability to do this work from a familiar, safe space, and the flexibility to fit therapy into a genuinely full life.

Real connection happens through a screen. Real breakthroughs happen on telehealth. And for women seeking specialized care that isn’t available locally, it opens access to something that might otherwise be out of reach.

I’m Alynne Davis, LCMHC, and I offer online therapy for women across North Carolina and South Carolina. I specialize in eating disorders, disordered eating, trauma, complex PTSD, ADHD in women, and body image — all through a trauma-informed, expressive arts approach that translates seamlessly to the virtual space.

Telehealth Might Be Right for You If…

Online therapy works well for many women — especially those seeking specialized support:

  • You’re not near Charlotte but want specialized eating disorder or trauma therapy
  • Your schedule doesn’t allow for commute time
  • You have young children at home and getting out isn’t always possible
  • You feel more comfortable doing this work in your own space
  • You live in a smaller NC or SC city where access to specialized therapists is limited
  • You travel for work and need flexibility in scheduling
  • You’re in postpartum recovery or managing a health condition that makes leaving home difficult

Wherever you are in North Carolina or South Carolina, you deserve access to therapy that’s actually specialized for what you’re navigating.

What Telehealth Therapy Looks Like

Secure, HIPAA-Compliant Platform

We meet via a secure, HIPAA-compliant video platform. All you need is a private space, a device with a camera, and a reliable internet connection. I’ll send you a link before each session — no complicated setup required.

50-Minute Sessions, Typically Weekly

Sessions are 50 minutes, typically weekly. The format is exactly the same as in-person therapy — a real, uninterrupted therapeutic conversation. The container is just as steady. The work is just as real.

Expressive Arts Therapy via Telehealth

Expressive arts therapy works beautifully via telehealth. Many clients do this work with simple materials they already have at home — a journal, colored pencils, whatever is available. If you don’t have anything, we find creative alternatives. The approach adapts naturally to the virtual format. Learn more about expressive arts therapy and how it works.

Licensed in NC and SC

I am licensed in both North Carolina and South Carolina, which means I can legally provide therapy to clients located in either state. If you’re in another state, I’m not able to see you — but I’m happy to help with referrals during a free consultation.

Specialties Available via Telehealth

The full range of my specializations is available to clients across NC and SC via telehealth:

  • Eating Disorder Therapy — anorexia, bulimia, binge eating, orthorexia, and disordered eating
  • Binge Eating Therapy — breaking the restrict-binge cycle and addressing the nervous system underneath
  • Trauma Therapy — complex PTSD, developmental trauma, relational trauma, and more
  • Body Image Therapy — healing from diet culture, body shame, and body-based distress
  • ADHD Therapy for Women — late-diagnosed ADHD, emotional dysregulation, and ADHD’s intersection with eating and mood
  • Expressive Arts Therapy — creative approaches integrated into any area of treatment

Insurance & Fees

I accept the following insurance plans for telehealth clients in NC and SC:

  • Aetna
  • Blue Cross Blue Shield (BCBS)
  • Carelon Behavioral Health
  • Cigna
  • Quest Behavioral Health

Out-of-pocket options are also available for clients who prefer not to use insurance or whose plan isn’t listed. A free 15-minute consultation is always available before you commit — it’s a chance to ask about fees, insurance, and anything else on your mind.

What Working Together Looks Like

Your First Session

Your first session is a space to tell your story. I’ll ask questions and listen closely, and we’ll begin to get a sense of what’s been happening and what you’re hoping for. There’s no checklist. Just a real conversation — from your space, at your pace.

Weekly Sessions

Sessions are 50 minutes, typically weekly. The format, the depth, and the quality of care are exactly the same via telehealth as in-person. Many clients find that the comfort of their own environment actually deepens the work. We show up fully — wherever we are.

Free 15-Minute Consultation

A free consultation is available before you commit. It’s a chance to ask questions, share a bit about where you are, and get a feel for whether we’re a good fit. Consultations happen via video or phone — whichever is easier for you. No forms, no pressure. Just a conversation.
