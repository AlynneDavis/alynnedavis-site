Deep Healing Sessions & Intensives

Some healing needs more than 50 minutes a week. Extended sessions and half-day intensives create the time and space for trauma work to actually move — without stopping just when you’re getting somewhere.

Schedule a Free Consultation

Trauma Doesn’t Fit in 50 Minutes

There’s something that happens in weekly therapy that can feel like running up to the edge of something real — and then having to stop, put it back, and wait another seven days to return to it. For some people, in some seasons, that pace is exactly right. But for others, it creates a kind of holding pattern. Real movement stays just out of reach.

Extended sessions and intensives work differently. When you have more time — to settle in, to let what’s there actually surface, to stay with it long enough for something to shift — the work can go somewhere it simply can’t in a standard session.

This isn’t about doing more in less time. It’s about giving your nervous system, your body, and your inner world the kind of sustained, uninterrupted container that deep healing actually requires.

You Might Be Ready for This Work If…

Weekly therapy feels too slow

You’re showing up consistently, you’re committed, but 50 minutes a week doesn’t feel like enough time to really get into it. You want to go deeper than the weekly format allows.

You’ve hit a wall

Progress has stalled. Something important isn’t moving, and you have a sense that what you need isn’t more of the same — you need something different. A longer, more immersive container might be exactly that.

Weekly sessions don’t fit your life

Your schedule, your location, or the cost of weekly therapy makes consistent sessions hard to sustain. Going deep less often — but in a much fuller way — might be a better fit for where you are right now.

You’re ready to go all in

You’re at a point in your life where you want to dedicate real time and energy to healing. Not chip away at it slowly. You’re ready to give yourself the space this work deserves — and you want a therapist who can hold that with you.

Two Ways to Work Together

Depending on where you are and what you need, there are two formats available.

Extended Sessions — 1.5 to 2 Hours

An extended session is a longer version of a standard therapy appointment — but what that extra time makes possible is significant. When you’re not watching the clock or bracing for the session to end, the nervous system can soften. You can go into something difficult and actually have enough time to come back out of it, to integrate, to feel settled before you leave.

Extended sessions work well for people who are in active trauma processing, who need more time to work with somatic or expressive arts approaches, or who simply find that 50 minutes always leaves them feeling like they’ve just gotten started.

  • Approximately 1.5–2 hours
  • In-person in Charlotte, NC or virtual throughout NC & SC
  • Private pay only — not covered by insurance

Half-Day Intensives — 3 to 4 Hours

A half-day intensive is a dedicated block of time — typically 3 to 4 hours, with breaks built in — designed for women who are ready to go deeper than weekly sessions allow. It creates a sustained, held container where real movement can happen within a single experience.

The intensive is tailored entirely to you. Before we meet, we’ll connect to understand what you’re carrying, what you’re hoping for, and what approaches are most likely to serve you. The day itself is collaborative, embodied, and paced with your nervous system in mind.

This format is especially well-suited to trauma processing, eating disorder and body image work, life transitions that feel stuck, and relational patterns that haven’t shifted in regular therapy.

  • Approximately 3–4 hours, with breaks
  • In-person in Charlotte, NC or virtual throughout NC & SC
  • Private pay only — not covered by insurance
  • Includes a pre-intensive consultation call

For new clients

All extended sessions and intensives with new clients begin with an initial intake session. This gives us the space to understand your history, what you’re carrying, and whether this format is the right fit — before we do any deep work together. Existing clients can move directly into extended sessions or intensives as clinically appropriate.

What This Work Actually Looks Like

This is not a talk therapy marathon. Extended sessions and intensives draw on a range of approaches — chosen based on what you need, not a fixed agenda.

Somatic & Body-Based Work

Trauma is stored in the body, not just the mind. Somatic approaches help you notice what your nervous system is holding, track sensation with curiosity rather than fear, and complete the cycles that trauma interrupted. The body knows things the mind hasn’t put into words yet.

Expressive Arts Processing

Painting, collage, movement, imagery, journaling — creative modalities access parts of your inner world that language alone can’t always reach. For trauma especially, where experiences often live before or below words, this kind of expression can open doors that talking circles around.

IFS — Parts Work

Internal Family Systems offers a way to understand the different parts of you that developed as responses to painful experiences — the part that protects, the part that shuts down, the part that holds the original wound. Parts work is compassionate, non-pathologizing, and deeply effective for complex trauma.

Integration & Closing

Every session ends with intentional integration — time to settle, to make meaning of what moved, and to leave with a sense of groundedness rather than openness. Deep work shouldn’t leave you raw and untethered. Closing well is part of the healing.

How This Connects to the Self-Discovery Intensive

Some clients begin with the Self-Discovery Intensive — a 3-session assessment process using the MARI® that creates a detailed map of your inner world, your patterns, and what you’re carrying. From there, deep healing sessions and intensives offer a natural next step: taking what was illuminated in the assessment into active, embodied processing.

Others come directly to extended sessions or intensives without having done the Self-Discovery Intensive first. Both are complete in themselves. If you’re not sure which is the right starting point for you, a free consultation call is the best place to figure it out together.

Important to Know

Private Pay Only

Extended sessions and half-day intensives are private pay and are not covered by insurance. This is a deliberate choice — the extended format, the depth of the work, and the ability to tailor each session entirely to your needs fall outside what insurance reimbursement allows.

A superbill can be provided upon request if you’d like to submit for potential out-of-network reimbursement. Pricing is discussed during our free consultation call. If you have questions about whether this format is within reach for you, please don’t hesitate to ask — I want the conversation to be honest and low-pressure.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a trauma therapy intensive?

A trauma therapy intensive is an extended therapy experience — either a longer individual session (1.5–2 hours) or a half-day block (3–4 hours) — designed to give trauma healing the time and space it actually needs. Rather than picking up and putting down difficult material week after week in 50-minute increments, an intensive allows you to go deeper, stay with what’s arising, and reach a place of real integration within a single session or day.

Is a trauma intensive right for me if I’m already in weekly therapy?

Yes. Some clients use extended sessions or intensives as a complement to their existing weekly therapy — either with me or with another therapist. Others choose the intensive format as their primary way of working because it fits their schedule, or because weekly sessions haven’t given them enough time to go as deep as they need. You don’t have to be in crisis, and you don’t have to have tried everything first. If you feel ready to do deeper work, that’s enough.

Do you accept insurance for deep healing sessions or intensives?

No. Extended sessions and half-day intensives are private pay only and are not covered by insurance. This is because the format — and the depth of work it allows — falls outside what insurance typically reimburses. A superbill can be provided upon request if you’d like to submit to your insurance for potential out-of-network reimbursement.

What does a half-day trauma intensive look like?

A half-day intensive typically runs 3–4 hours, with breaks built in. The session is tailored to you — what you’re carrying, what you’re ready for, and what modalities will serve you best. This might include somatic and body-based work, expressive arts processing (painting, collage, movement, journaling), IFS parts work, and relational or narrative processing. The goal is not to push or overwhelm — it’s to give you a sustained container where real movement becomes possible.

Are deep healing sessions available via telehealth in North Carolina?

Yes. Both extended sessions and half-day intensives are available virtually for clients throughout North Carolina and South Carolina. In-person sessions are available in Charlotte, NC. Some clients prefer in-person for intensive work because of the grounding that physical presence offers; others find the comfort of their own space supportive. We can talk through what’s likely to work best for you during our consultation call.

Alynne Davis, LCMHC offers therapy in person in Charlotte, NC and via telehealth throughout NC and SC — including Raleigh, Asheville, Durham, Wilmington, Columbia SC, and Greenville SC.
